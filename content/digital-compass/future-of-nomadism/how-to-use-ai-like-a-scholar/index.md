## Prologue: The Smell of Cedarwood and Curiosity
My father’s library in Ksar El-Kébir reeked of cedarwood oil and curiosity. Stacked between ancient Amazigh scrolls and dog-eared H.G. Wells novels, I stumbled upon Ibn Khaldun’s *Muqaddimah* at 14. His observation—“*The past clings to the future like ivy on a crumbling wall*”—became my compass. Today, as tech gurus preach “disruption,” I whisper to my laptop: *“Tqelleb, tqelleb, tqelleb”* (Turn, turn, turn)—the mantra of zellige artisans outside my childhood window.  

This isn’t anti-tech. This is *Rooted Nomadism*: **where algorithms knead cultural clay instead of erasing it**. This is the story of how I began to use AI like a 15th-Century Moroccan scholar, starting with how my grandmother’s tile philosophy hacked ChatGPT.

## 1. When AI Met Zellige: The 2006 Cybercafe Epiphany

I ignored him—until ChatGPT spat out product descriptions calling Moroccan clay "exotic primal mud."  

**My Fix:**  
- Fed AI my grandmother’s notebook: *“Ghassoul clay purifies like mountain spring rituals.”*  
- Trained it on 12th-century Andalusian poetry: *“Bathe in earth, emerge reborn.”*  
- **Result**: *Ritual Hammam*’s blog now reads like a love letter to the Atlas Mountains.  

> *“Code with clay, not plastic—your tools should echo your terrain.”*
