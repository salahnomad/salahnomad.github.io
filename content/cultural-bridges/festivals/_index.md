A festival is a city's soul made visible. It's a temporary republic where the normal rules are suspended and a deeper, more ancient rhythm takes over. For a brief, intense period, a community comes together to perform its stories, celebrate its values, and remember what it means to belong to something larger than oneself.

This section is an exploration of these open-air stages. From the theatrical chaos of the Avignon Festival to the sacred rhythms of a Moroccan *moussem*, we discover how these moments of collective effervescence hold profound lessons for building authentic communities, both online and off.
