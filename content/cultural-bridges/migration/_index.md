Exile isn't erasure; it is transplantation. To be uprooted is not to disappear, but to become compost for new ways of belonging. The story of humanity is a story of migration, a constant ebb and flow of peoples carrying their worlds within them like a snail carries its shell.

This section delves into the rich, complex experiences of diaspora. We uncover the ancestral wisdom encoded in the trauma of displacement, and discover how the act of carrying one's home in memory forges a unique and powerful form of resilience. This is a tribute to the citizens of the in-between, the eternal navigators of straits both physical and spiritual.
